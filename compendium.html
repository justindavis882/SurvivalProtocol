01. CORE ENGINE & COMBAT

SURVIVAL PROTOCOL is powered by a high-tension 2d6 engine. The world is unforgiving, and inventory management is life or death.

The 2d6 Engine

Every check requires rolling two six-sided dice (2d6) and adding the relevant Stat bonus (Brains, Strength, Reputation). The Overseer sets the Target Number (TN) for success.

  • TN 7: Easy Tasks (Barricading an unlocked door).
  • TN 9: Normal Tasks (Scavenging an unlooted store).
  • TN 11: Hard Tasks (Hotwiring a car under fire).

Advantage: Roll 3d6, keep the two highest.
Disadvantage: Roll 3d6, keep the two lowest.

Criticals & Fumbles: Double 6s equals an automatic critical success (doubles base weapon damage in combat). Double 1s (snake eyes) is an automatic critical failure.

Combat & The 2-Action Economy

Combat uses Group Initiative. Survivors roll 2d6 + Brains. The Overseer rolls 2d6 for the Horde. Highest roll goes first, and the winning faction takes all their turns in any order.

During combat, a player has exactly 2 Actions per turn. Everything except talking costs an action: Moving, firing a gun, reloading, wrapping a bandage, or drawing a weapon from the Pack.

Weapon Damage: Damage is flat. Light Melee deals 1 Base Damage, Sidearms deal 2 Base Damage, Long Guns deal 3 Base Damage.

02. STEALTH & THE DECIBEL GAUGE

Zombies operate on the "Proximity Threat" rule. They attack the closest loud noise. The Overseer tracks this using the 6-segment Decibel Gauge.

The Horde Ambush: When the 6th segment of the gauge is filled, it "Pegs Out." The Overseer immediately interrupts the turn order, clears the gauge, and drops Walkers into immediate close combat with the players.

03. SCAVENGING & CRAFTING

Players roll 2d6 + Brains to search a location. Success yields broad Item Tags rather than specific items (e.g., [MEDICAL], [AUTO]). During downtime, the Overseer rolls on Drop Tables to determine the exact loot.

Salvaging Raw Materials

Players can break down broad tags at a Workbench to craft blueprints. Standard conversion rates:

  • [HARDWARE] -> 2 Mechanical, Wood, or Scrap Metal.
  • [MEDICAL] -> 2 Chemical, Plastic, or Glass.
  • [FOOD] -> 2 Biomat or Water.
  • [AUTO] -> 2 Scrap Metal or Battery.

04. THE SAFEHOUSE & UPKEEP

The core loop of the game revolves around returning to the Safehouse and surviving the Upkeep Phase.

  • Consumption: Deduct 1 unit of Food/Water for every living person in the Safehouse per day.
  • Condition Critical: If a player's Health, Stamina, or Food bar hits 0, they mark a "Condition Critical" penalty box, suffering a cumulative -1 to ALL rolls.
  • Structural Decay: Safehouses passively lose 5% Integrity every in-game week. If Integrity reaches 0%, a Horde Ambush triggers automatically during the next rest phase.
  • Coma & Permadeath: If Health hits 0, a Coma Timer begins. If not healed in 3 combat rounds (or 1 narrative minute), the character dies permanently. The player must roll a new survivor or adopt an NPC from the base.

05. ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS

The climate actively attacks Condition bars and changes the tactical rules of the grid.

  • Winter: Moving costs double Stamina. Reaching 0 Stamina triggers immediate frostbite damage. Safehouses require [FUEL] daily, or resting restores no Health.
  • Summer: Combat and sprinting cost Food/Water bubbles instead of just daily consumption. Unpreserved food spoils in 3 days.
  • Monsoons & Flooding: Imposes Disadvantage on navigation/perception. Moving in waist-deep water costs 2 Stamina. Firearms have a 1-in-6 chance to jam when fired.
  • Absolute Blackout: Moving in the dark imposes a -2 penalty. Igniting light sources removes the penalty but generates +1 Decibel Tick every 10 in-game minutes.

07. BARTER & FACTIONS

Ammunition is the standard currency of the apocalypse. When dealing with neutral factions, players roll 2d6 + Reputation to negotiate. A failure (6 or less) doubles prices or demands upfront collateral; a critical success (10+) halves them.

  • 1 Scrap / Fabric: 1 Value CR
  • 1 Can of Food / Handgun Bullet: 2 Value CR
  • 1 Rifle Round: 3 Value CR
  • 1 [MEDICAL] or [AUTO] Tag: 5 Value CR

08. VEHICLE OPERATIONS

Vehicles require fuel to operate and have their own Integrity track (0-100%).

  • Trunk Grid: Vehicles possess expanded Inventory Grids (e.g., 30 slots for a truck bed) allowing for massive loot hauling.
  • Ramming: The Driver can spend actions to roll Strength or Brains to ram obstacles or hordes. Success deals the vehicle's Base Ram Damage. Failure causes the vehicle to bog down and lose 10% Integrity.
  • Noise: Driving a combustion engine vehicle completely ignores the Decibel Gauge—the noise is already absolute, meaning the Horde will naturally migrate toward the vehicle's hex.
