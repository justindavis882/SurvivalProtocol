00. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

THE WORLD IS GONE. WHAT REMAINS IS THE SAFE HAVEN.

SURVIVAL PROTOCOL is a gritty, resource-heavy tabletop roleplaying game powered by a high-tension 2d6 engine. You are not a superhero. You are a survivor holding onto the ragged edge of humanity.

In this system, ammunition is currency, a loud noise is a death sentence, and the walls of your Safehouse are the only things keeping the horde at bay.

THE GOLDEN RULES

  • Always Roll 2d6: Every action is resolved by rolling two six-sided dice, adding your Stat Bonus, and meeting or beating the GM's Target Number.
  • Criticals and Fumbles: Rolling double 6s is an automatic critical success (in combat, this doubles base damage). Rolling double 1s is an automatic critical failure, regardless of your stat bonuses.
  • Two Actions Only: During combat, you have exactly two actions. Firing a gun, wrapping a bandage, running away—everything costs an action.
  • Inventory is Tetris: If it doesn't fit in your Loadout Grid, you can't carry it.
  • Death is Real: If your Health hits zero and you are not rescued in 3 rounds, your character is gone. The game continues. You simply adopt another survivor from the Safehouse.

SECTION TWO // MASTER DIRECTIVES

SYSTEM RULES &
PLAYING GUIDES

1. CORE ENGINE & COMBAT

THE 2D6 ENGINE

Every check requires rolling two six-sided dice (2d6) and adding the relevant Stat bonus. The GM sets the Target Number (TN) for success based on difficulty.

  • 7 = Easy
  • 9 = Normal
  • 11 = Hard

Criticals and Fumbles: Rolling double 6s is an automatic critical success. In combat, this doubles the base damage. Rolling double 1s (snake eyes) is an automatic critical failure.

ADVANTAGE & DISADVANTAGE

Some gear, environments, or tactical situations alter your odds.
Advantage: Roll 3d6 and keep the two highest dice.
Disadvantage: Roll 3d6 and keep the two lowest dice.

THE CONDITION PENALTY

If any Condition Bar (Health, Stamina, Food) reaches zero, check a Condition Critical penalty box. You take a cumulative -1 to all rolls per checked box (max -3).

COMBAT INITIATIVE

Combat uses Group Initiative. Roll 2d6 + Brains for the Survivors. The GM rolls 2d6 + Threat for Humans, or a flat 2d6 for the Zombie Horde. The highest roll goes first, and all members of that faction take their turns in any order they choose.

THE 2-ACTION ECONOMY

During combat, a player has exactly 2 Actions per turn. Everything except talking costs an action. You can attack twice, move twice, or mix actions (e.g., Reload + Shoot).

WEAPON DAMAGE & MODIFICATIONS

Weapons deal flat base damage to keep combat brutally fast. However, these base stats can be boosted by crafting and attaching accessories or mods to a weapon's Modification Nodes.

  • Light Melee / Unarmed: 1 Base Dmg (Adjacent)
  • Heavy Melee: 2 Base Dmg (Adjacent)
  • Sidearm: 2 Base Dmg (Close)
  • Long Gun / Special: 3 Base Dmg (Far)

2. SCAVENGING & SAFEHOUSE

THE LOADOUT GRID

Inventory is tracked visually. The Rig holds Quick Access items (0 actions to retrieve in combat). The Pack holds Deep Storage items (requires 1 action to retrieve). Weapons take up slots and feature visual checkboxes for ammo and durability.

SCAVENGING & TAGS

Roll 2d6 + Brains to scavenge a location. Success yields broad Item Tags (e.g., [Medical], [Auto], [Food]). The GM consults drop tables to determine exact usable items.

SALVAGING RAW MATERIALS

Crafting recipes often require specific raw materials. During downtime, you can break down your broad scavenging tags at a Workbench to yield specific crafting components:

  • [HARDWARE] breaks down into 2 [MECHANICAL], [WOOD], or [SCRAP METAL]
  • [MEDICAL] breaks down into 2 [CHEMICAL], [PLASTIC], or [GLASS]
  • [FOOD] breaks down into 2 [BIOMAT] or [WATER]
  • [AUTO] breaks down into 2 [SCRAP METAL] or [BATTERY]
  • [WEAPONS & AMMO] breaks down into 2 [EXPLOSIVE] or [SCRAP METAL]

THE SAFEHOUSE INTEGRITY

Tracked from 0% to 100%. A building must reach 75% Integrity to be a secure HQ. Safehouses passively lose 5% Integrity every in-game week unless maintained.

THE DOWNTIME PHASE

When resting, time is tracked on the Watch Dial. You get 1 Project Action (crafting, repairing) per 4 hours of rest. Staying past 12 hours drains Food reserves and triggers Threat Checks from the GM.

THE DECIBEL GAUGE (STEALTH)

The GM tracks noise on a 6-segment dial. Failed stealth rolls add 1 tick. Unsuppressed guns add 3 ticks. Shotguns instantly fill the dial. When full, a Horde Ambush triggers instantly.

NEXT SURVIVOR PROTOCOL

If you die (via Coma or Infection), you immediately adopt a recruited NPC from the Safehouse Roster. They start at half the level of your deceased character. If the base is empty, roll a new Wanderer at Level 1.

3. THREAT & MUTATION INDEX

ZOMBIES ALWAYS ATTACK THE CLOSEST LOUD NOISE OR THE CLOSEST PIECE OF MEAT.

SPECIMENHEALTHDAMAGETACTICAL TRAIT
Grapple: If two or more Walkers are engaged with a player, that player must automatically spend 1 Action to shove them off before they can move, attack, or use items.
THE RUNNER (Pursuit)31Relentless: Runners ignore standard movement rules. They can sprint across an entire combat zone AND attack in the same turn. Escaping costs Stamina.
THE SPITTER (Artillery)21 (Ranged)Corrosive Bile: Instead of normal damage, a Spitter's attack forces the player to either check an Infection box OR destroy one Durability Bubble on their gear.
THE GRONK (Juggernaut)83Thick Skull: Immune to Light Melee weapons. Gronks deal massive structural damage—destroying 10% Safehouse Integrity per strike.

INFECTION TIMELINE

If infected (via Spitter or GM narrative event), track progress on the Vitals page.

  • Dawn: Check 1 Infection Box at the start of each in-game day.
  • Suppressants: Consuming `[Medical]` suppressants clears 1 Box, buying 24 hours.
  • Termination: If the 3rd box is checked, the character dies permanently.

4. ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS & BARTER

SEASONAL SHIFTS

The climate actively attacks Condition bars.

  • Winter: Moving costs double Stamina. Reaching 0 Stamina triggers immediate Health drain (frostbite). Safehouses require `[Fuel]` daily, or resting restores no Health.
  • Summer: Combat and sprinting cost Food/Water bubbles instead of just daily consumption.
  • Monsoons: -1 penalty to all Scavenging checks. Firearms have a 1-in-6 chance to jam.

NIGHT OPERATIONS

Exploring in the dark inflicts a -2 penalty to 2d6 rolls. Ranged attacks beyond "Close" are impossible.

Light Sources: Flashlights or flares remove the penalty but automatically add +1 Tick to the Decibel Gauge every turn they remain on.

THE BARTER ECONOMY

When dealing with neutral human factions, Ammunition is the standard currency. Roll 2d6 + Reputation to negotiate. A failure doubles prices; a critical success halves them.

  • 1 Scrap / Fabric: 1 Value
  • 1 Can of Food: 2 Value
  • 1 Handgun / Shotgun Shell: 2 Value
  • 1 Rifle Round: 3 Value
  • 1 Medical / Auto Tag: 5 Value
